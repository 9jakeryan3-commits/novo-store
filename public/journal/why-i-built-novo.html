Why I Built NoVo

I didn't build NoVo because I found a magic strategy. I built it because I finally admitted the strategy was never my problem. I was.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026
Part of the 0DTE SPY Options Scalping guide →

Like a lot of people, I came to trading thinking the hard part was figuring out what to do — the right setup, the right indicator, the right edge. I read everything, I tested everything, and eventually I had setups that genuinely worked. And I still gave money back to the market. Not because the plan was wrong — because the moment real money was on the line, I stopped following it.

I hesitated on entries I'd already decided to take. I widened a stop to "give it room" instead of accepting the loss I'd planned for. I forced a trade after a red morning to win it back. I'd be sharp for an hour and sloppy by the afternoon. The plan was fine. The human executing it — me — was the leak.

The strategy was never the hard part. Doing the same disciplined thing, every single time, under pressure — that's the part humans break on. I know, because I broke on it.

I tried the obvious fixes

Signal services? They just handed the work back to me — I still had to size it, place it, and hold the stop, which was exactly where I failed. Rule bots were rigid and went stale the moment the market changed character. Copy bots wanted account access or my money, which I wasn't willing to give a stranger. Nothing actually closed the gap between a good plan and a disciplined execution of it.

So I built the thing I wished existed

I wanted a system that did the whole job — read the market continuously, found the trade, sized it to conviction, executed without hesitation, and exited on a strict ladder I set — and never, ever revenge-traded or moved a stop out of hope. Not a tool that handed the trading back to me — one that ran the discipline I couldn't sustain by hand, every time, exactly the way I set it.

That's NoVo. It does the part I'm bad at — the consistent, emotionless execution — and leaves me the part I should own: setting the risk I'm comfortable with.

The one rule I refused to break

There's a reason NoVo is non-custodial, and it's personal: I would never hand my own money to a black box and hope. So NoVo doesn't ask you to. It connects to your own brokerage account through your own keys and only places trades — it never holds, moves, or touches your funds. I built it the way I'd want to use it, because I do use it.

What I won't tell you

I won't show you screenshots of impossible returns or promise you'll make money — no honest system can, and anyone who does is selling you something else. Trading carries real risk, and NoVo has losing trades and losing days like any system. What I will promise is discipline and transparency: NoVo runs your rules without flinching, and this whole journal exists to show you exactly how it works — no black box, no hand-waving.

If you've ever watched yourself blow up a good plan in real time, NoVo was built for you. It was built by someone who did exactly that — and got tired of it.
