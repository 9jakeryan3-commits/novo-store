When Should You Turn On Auto-Entry? The Honest Checklist

Auto-entry isn't good or bad — it's situational. The honest question isn't “should I use it?” but “is this the kind of situation where handing over the entry actually makes sense?”

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

NoVo's auto-entry is opt-in because it genuinely fits some situations and not others. Rather than a blanket yes or no, here's an honest checklist for deciding when turning it on makes sense — and when your click should stay in the loop.

When auto-entry can make sense

Consider it when: your setup is genuinely mechanical — a clearly-defined, rules-based entry you've validated, where discretion adds little; you can't watch the screen and would rather a defined rule act than miss the setup entirely (accepting the tradeoff); your discretion is hurting you — if you hesitate, revenge trade, or override good rules, a mechanical entry can enforce discipline your emotions won't; or conditions are calm and rule-friendly, not headline-driven chaos.

When to keep your click in the loop

Keep it manual when: context matters — catalyst days, headline risk, choppy or unusual regimes where rules go blind; your edge is discretionary — if reading real-time context is how you make money, don't automate away your edge; or you're still learning — you want the reps of making the call yourself. When in doubt, default to manual; that's why NoVo does.

Auto-entry fits a mechanical setup in calm conditions, or a trader whose discretion hurts them. It does not fit a discretionary edge on a headline day. Match the tool to the situation.

The honest guidance

Start manual, always — learn the cockpit and your own decision-making first. Consider auto-entry only for specific, well-understood, mechanical situations, and know exactly what you're trading away (your contextual judgment) for what you're getting (hands-off convenience or enforced discipline). And whatever you choose, your boundaries still bind: even in auto, NoVo won't trade outside the limits you set. Auto-entry is a tool for the right moment, not a default to flip on and forget.
