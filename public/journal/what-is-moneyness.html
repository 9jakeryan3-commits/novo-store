Options 101

What Is Moneyness?

Moneyness is the single word for “where’s my strike relative to the price?” — and the answer shapes everything about how the option behaves.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

Moneyness describes where an option’s strike sits relative to the underlying price — in-the-money, at-the-money, or out-of-the-money. It drives an option’s cost, delta, and behavior.

The three states

In-the-money (ITM): has intrinsic value (call strike below price, put above), higher delta, more expensive. At-the-money (ATM): strike near the current price — maximum time value and gamma, ~0.50 delta. Out-of-the-money (OTM): no intrinsic value, cheap, low delta, needs a move to pay (see ITM vs OTM).

Why it matters

Moneyness determines responsiveness (ITM tracks the underlying, OTM lags), cost (ITM pricey, OTM cheap), and odds (ITM more likely to finish with value). For a scalp, you generally want near-the-money moneyness — responsive and liquid. It’s the first thing to consider when picking a strike.

Moneyness is where your strike stands relative to price — and it sets the option’s cost, speed, and odds all at once.

The takeaway

Moneyness (ITM/ATM/OTM) is the foundational descriptor of an option’s position and behavior. For scalping, near-the-money is the responsive sweet spot; far-OTM is the lottery trap. It’s inseparable from strike selection.
