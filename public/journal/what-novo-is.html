The System

What NoVo Actually Is

It gets called a "0DTE bot." It isn't one. NoVo is a multi-layer, autonomous SPY options execution system with a decay curve you control — and the difference matters.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026
Part of the 0DTE SPY Options Scalping guide →

The fastest way to misunderstand NoVo is to file it next to the two things most people already know: the "trading bot" that follows a fixed rule sheet, and the "signal service" that pings your phone and leaves the trading to you. NoVo is neither.

Start with the biggest misconception

NoVo is not locked to same-day expiry. It trades SPY options at a DTE — days to expiration — that you choose, from same-day (0DTE) out to several days, with next-day (1DTE) as the sensible default. 0DTE is one setting on a dial, not the whole product. Same-day expiry is the fastest, most aggressive mode; next-day gives the same rapid decay with a one-day buffer; a few days out gives a position more room to work. You turn the dial to match your risk appetite.

So the honest one-line description is: NoVo autonomously trades SPY options at the expiration you choose, and manages the entire trade for you — entry to exit.

Not a rule-sheet bot

A basic bot does one thing: "if indicator X crosses Y, buy." That works until the market changes character, and then it bleeds. NoVo is a layered system that weighs several independent reads of the market at once before it ever commits — what the live tape is doing, how options dealers are positioned, what the broader macro backdrop looks like, where retail emotion is sitting, and what the chart structure says. No single trigger fires a trade on its own.

Not a signal feed

A signal service leaves the hard part to you. It tells you what it sees and then steps back — leaving you to size the position, place the order, manage the stop, and decide when to get out, under pressure. That's exactly where discipline breaks down. NoVo closes that gap: working inside the rules and risk limits you set, it finds the trade, sizes it, routes the order, manages the position, and exits — automatically, so the discipline doesn't hinge on you executing flawlessly by hand. You set the plan and the boundaries; the tool handles the mechanical execution.

Most tools give you a sharper cockpit and still make you fly the plane. NoVo flies the plane.

The layers, in plain English

You don't need to operate any of this — it runs itself — but here's what's under the hood:

  • A live market read. NoVo watches the SPY tape continuously and tracks how options dealers are positioned, building a real-time picture of pressure and structure rather than a lagging chart snapshot.
  • A conviction score. Those reads are fused into a single measure of how strong a setup is right now, which also scales how much it commits to the trade.
  • An AI vision layer. On a regular cadence, NoVo has an AI model analyze live market structure — trend, key levels, momentum — and produce a directional bias that gates entries.
  • A macro and psychology read. Separate AI layers classify the broader regime (risk-on, risk-off, chop) and model where retail traders are likely trapped or about to capitulate.
  • Adaptive execution. When it's time to fill an order, NoVo chooses among several execution approaches and learns over time which fills best in which conditions.
  • A strict exit hierarchy. Hard stop, trailing stops, and staged take-profits — all configurable by you, with no hidden discretionary exits.
  • A memory that compounds. Every trade is recorded with full context, and once a week NoVo distills recent outcomes into written rules that shape its future behavior.

A few always-on services, one machine

Under the hood NoVo runs as a small set of always-on services: the trading engine, the market-data pipeline, and the web dashboard you control it from. They share a fast in-memory state so the data feed never blocks the trading engine, and the trading engine never waits on the AI. It runs around the clock so you don't have to — reach your dashboard from any browser or your phone, anywhere.

What you actually touch

You interact with all of it through the C2 Dashboard — a web app you can open in any browser or add to your phone's home screen. From there you authorize trading, halt it instantly, set your risk parameters, watch live positions, and read the system's own market commentary. The complexity stays inside; the controls are simple.

That's NoVo: not a bot, not a signal feed, but an execution system that does the whole job — on the instrument it was built for, at the speed you can't match, with the discipline you can't sustain.
