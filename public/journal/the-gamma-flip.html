The Gamma Flip: Where the Market's Behavior Inverts

There's a price level where the market stops absorbing moves and starts amplifying them. Cross it, and the whole session can change character.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

If you've read our guide to gamma exposure, you know dealer hedging can either dampen moves or amplify them depending on whether dealers are net long or short gamma. The gamma flip is the price where that balance crosses zero — the dividing line between the two worlds.

A quick recap of the two regimes

In positive gamma, dealers sell rallies and buy dips to stay neutral — stabilizing flow that pins price and suppresses volatility. In negative gamma, the hedging inverts: dealers buy rallies and sell dips, adding fuel to moves. Same market, opposite behavior, decided by the sign of net gamma.

The zero-gamma level

The gamma flip (or “zero-gamma level”) is simply the price at which net dealer gamma is zero — the boundary between those regimes. Above it, the market tends to behave like it has shock absorbers. Below it, it behaves like it's on ice. The level itself isn't fixed; it shifts as positioning, price, and time change through the session.

Above the flip, breakouts get absorbed. Below it, the same breakout accelerates. The line decides which market you're trading.

Why crossing it matters

The practical value isn't a precise trigger — it's a heads-up about character. When price is comfortably above the flip, fading extremes and expecting mean reversion is the more natural posture. When price breaks below it, chasing momentum and respecting trend makes more sense, because the hedging is now amplifying every push. Trading a mean-reversion playbook in a negative-gamma tape is how good setups turn into fast losses.

It's a map, not a magic line

An important caveat: the gamma flip is an estimate, reconstructed from options data, not a published number stamped on the chart. It's a probabilistic map of the terrain, not a guaranteed pivot. Treat it as context that tilts the odds, not a level that must hold.
