The Best Broker for 0DTE Options: How to Actually Decide

Most “best broker” lists are affiliate rankings. Here’s the honest version: the criteria that actually matter for 0DTE, so you can pick the right one for you.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

Educational only, not financial advice. Broker features, options-approval requirements, 0DTE availability, and fees change and vary by account — verify current details on the broker’s own site. NoVo is independent and not affiliated with the brokers named.

There’s no single “best broker” for 0DTE options — the right one depends on your priorities. Instead of an affiliate ranking, here are the criteria that actually matter, so you can decide.

What actually matters for 0DTE

Options approval & 0DTE access — can you get the approval level to buy options, and are same-day expirations available on your account? Cost — commissions and fees per contract add up fast when you trade often (see cheapest broker for scalping). Execution & platform speed — fast, reliable order entry matters on a fast tape. Liquidity/routing — clean fills on liquid strikes. The PDT rule — under $25k, all brokers apply the same three-day-trade limit, so factor that in.

Match the broker to how you trade

Newer traders often value a simple interface and commission-free trades (Robinhood, Webull, moomoo); options-focused traders often prefer platforms with deeper tools (tastytrade, Schwab/thinkorswim, Power E*TRADE); cost-focused traders often look at Interactive Brokers. There’s no universal winner — verify each broker’s current approval, fees, and 0DTE availability against your needs.

The “best broker” is the one that fits your fees, your platform comfort, and your approval — not the one with the biggest affiliate payout on a listicle.

The broker isn’t your edge

One honest reframe: your broker choice matters far less than your process and discipline. The best broker won’t save a bad strategy, and a good trader can win on a modest platform.
