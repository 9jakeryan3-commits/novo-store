What Are Stock Market Circuit Breakers?

Circuit breakers are the market’s emergency brakes — rare, dramatic, and worth understanding before the day they fire.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

Educational only, not financial advice. Market rules and thresholds can change — verify current specifics with the exchanges or your broker.

Circuit breakers are automatic trading halts triggered by severe market declines — pausing the whole market to prevent panic-driven crashes. They’re rare but dramatic, and they matter to anyone trading SPY.

How the levels work

Market-wide circuit breakers key off single-day drops in the S&P 500 at three thresholds: Level 1 (7%), Level 2 (13%), and Level 3 (20%). Level 1 or 2 before 3:25pm ET halts all trading for 15 minutes; the same drop at/after 3:25pm doesn’t halt. Level 3 at any time halts trading for the rest of the day. The idea is to force a pause so panic can cool.

Why they exist

Circuit breakers were introduced after past crashes to slow runaway selling and give the market time to find buyers — a deliberate speed bump against cascading, algorithm-fueled declines. They’re distinct from single-stock LULD halts (which pause one security) — circuit breakers halt everything.

Circuit breakers are the market’s panic button: a 7%, 13%, or 20% S&P drop and the whole market stops to breathe.

What it means for a scalper

You’ll rarely see one, but if a Level 1 hits, trading pauses and your open positions are frozen mid-move — a real risk on a crash day (and a reason to size for the worst case and respect shock risk). On such days, dealer gamma is usually deeply negative and moves are violent. Know they exist so a halt doesn’t blindside you.
