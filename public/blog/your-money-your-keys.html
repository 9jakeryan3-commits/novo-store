Your Money, Your Keys

Ask the hard question of any "AI trading" product first: where does my money actually live? For most of them, the answer should make you walk away. For NoVo, it never leaves your own broker.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

The automated-trading world splits cleanly into two camps. One camp hands you a sharper cockpit — better charts, scanners, alerts — and still makes you fly the plane. The other camp flies the plane for you, but only if you wire your money into their platform and trust a black box you can't see inside.

NoVo refuses both deals. It's fully autonomous and fully yours. That combination is rare, and it's deliberate.

Non-custodial, by design

NoVo never takes custody of your funds. Your money sits in your own brokerage account, in your name, at a regulated US broker. NoVo connects to that account through the broker's own official API using keys that you generate and that live on your machine. It places trades on your behalf — and that's all it can do. It can't withdraw, it can't transfer, it can't move your money anywhere. There's no NoVo wallet, no "deposit to get started," no pooled fund.

Compare that to the typical autonomous "AI fund," where step one is sending your capital into their system. The day you do that, you've traded control for convenience and taken on counterparty risk on top of market risk. With NoVo, the only thing that ever leaves your hands is a trade order.

Autonomous shouldn't mean handing over the keys. With NoVo, the machine does the trading — but the account, the funds, and the keys stay yours.

It runs on your machine

NoVo isn't a website you log into where the real engine runs somewhere you can't see. The system runs on your own Windows PC. You can watch it work, halt it with one button, and pull the keys at any moment. There's no intermediary deciding what happens to your account — just software you run, executing rules you set, against a broker you chose.

Transparency you can audit

Trust isn't a slogan; it's something you should be able to verify. NoVo keeps a complete, local trade journal — every entry and exit it has ever made, with the context behind each one. You can see exactly what it did and why, on your own dashboard, in your own database, exportable any time. Its live market commentary is written out in plain language so you can follow its reasoning in real time. Nothing about your trading history is locked away on someone else's server.

Why this is the moat

In a space crowded with screenshots of impossible returns and platforms that quietly vanish with people's deposits, the most valuable thing a trading product can offer isn't a flashier promise — it's the absence of one. NoVo's pitch is almost boring by comparison: we don't touch your money, and we don't hide what we're doing.

That honesty cuts both ways, and it should. Because your money never leaves your control, the outcomes — good and bad — are fully yours too. NoVo runs the trading hands-off; you still own the result, and you should understand what it's doing and accept that it has losing days like any real system. That's not a disclaimer we bury. It's the relationship: a powerful tool you control, not a black box you hope in.
