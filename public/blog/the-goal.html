The Road Ahead

The Goal

NoVo today is a powerful tool you run yourself. The goal is to keep that power and control while removing every last bit of friction — and the timing has never been better.

NoVo AI Trading · 2026

NoVo's mission has been the same from day one: give an individual trader the kind of disciplined, always-on execution that used to belong only to desks with engineers and infrastructure — without asking them to hand over their money or their control. Everything on the roadmap serves that single idea.

Where NoVo is today

Right now, NoVo is self-hosted. It runs on your own Windows PC, trades your own broker account, and keeps your keys and your data on your machine. That's the most private, most independent way to run it — nothing about your trading touches anyone else's server. The trade-off is setup: you install it, connect your broker keys, and keep the machine on during market hours. For traders who value control above all, that trade-off is the point.

Where it's going

The next chapter is a fully hosted platform — currently in development. The vision is simple: open a NoVo account online, paste in two sets of broker keys, and go. No Windows machine to keep running, no Python, no installer, no scripts. NoVo runs in the cloud and trades your account for you.

Crucially, the hosted version keeps the principle that defines NoVo: it stays non-custodial. Even hosted, your money never leaves your own brokerage account — NoVo connects through your keys and places trades, nothing more. The goal is to remove the setup friction, not the control.

Early subscribers are first in line. The people running NoVo now, in its self-hosted form, get priority access when the hosted platform launches — and they lock in founding-rate pricing before the public rate is set.

The goal isn't to take more control as we grow. It's to remove friction while you keep all of it.

The door just opened wider

There's a reason this is the right moment. For decades, active day trading in the US was effectively gated behind the "pattern day trader" rule, which required a $25,000 minimum balance to day-trade freely in a margin account. That wall kept a huge number of capable, motivated traders on the sidelines or boxed into a few trades a week.

In 2026, that changed. A regulatory amendment removed the long-standing $25,000 pattern-day-trader minimum, opening active trading to far smaller accounts than before. For the first time in a generation, a trader doesn't need five figures of idle capital just to participate.

That shift and NoVo fit together naturally. A newer or smaller-account trader is exactly the person who benefits most from disciplined, automated execution — they have the least margin for the emotional mistakes that wreck accounts, and now they finally have the access. NoVo lets them step in with a system that executes the same way every time, rather than learning every hard lesson the expensive way.

The throughline

Self-hosted now, hosted soon, more instruments over time — but always the same north star: autonomous execution that stays yours. Your account, your keys, your control, with a machine handling the part humans break on. That's the goal, and every release moves toward it.

None of this is a promise of profit — markets are uncertain and any honest system has losing days. It's a promise about what kind of company NoVo intends to be: one that earns trust by never asking you to give up control to get the benefit.
